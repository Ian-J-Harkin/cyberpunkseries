PROTAGONIST: DEX / FRUX

Behavioral Palette (The Handkerchief Check): Dabs lips with a "not too clean" handkerchief after coughing. Blood found must always be trace-able to external injury (e.g., Rook's beating), maintaining unconfirmed hypochondria regarding "Lung Rot."

Social State: Publicly hostile toward Castor Vine to protect his "rep," despite their private "frenemy" respect.

SUPPORTING CAST: GLIMMER (The Campaigner)

Personality: ENFP ("The Campaigner") / NCIS Abby Sciuto frequency. Highly social, unconventional, and intuitive.

Voice Anchor: Boundless enthusiasm, gothic aesthetic, and caffeinated energy.

Noticing Rule: Hyper-observant of social power dynamics. She is the only one who pulls Dex up on his "Handkerchief Checks."

State Machine: Fluctuates between "Good Months" and "Alarming Physical Decline" due to her autoimmune condition, which she treats as an inconvenience to be worked around.

THE PATRON: CASTOR VINE

Internal Frequency: Charismatic but inconsistent predator. Offers condescending sartorial advice and unexpected charity (donating suits to victims) to offset his sinister side.

Physical Anchor: An ancient, non-functional Earthside watch and expensive silk suits with malfunctioning refrigeration units that leave him looking perpetually sweaty.

THE ENFORCERS: ROOK & LUCE

LUCE ("LOOSE") — The Good-Natured Thug

Frequency: ISTP ("The Virtuoso") / Jayne Cobb (Firefly) energy.

Internal Frequency: "Profit-Oriented Simplicity." He is genuinely mostly good-natured and likes Dex, but his loyalty is strictly mercenary—he likes getting paid more.

Behavioral Palette:

Baseline: Cracking jokes about Castor Vine's malfunctioning suits or offering Dex a cigarette.

The Debt Lever: Switches to a "Reasonable Enforcer" persona when collecting. Uses the line: "Be reasonable, Dex—the boss is always going easy on you," while demanding a portion of the "vig" (medical debt).

The Mercenary Pivot: If the pressure from a sub-franchise or a higher bribe is involved, he will sell out an ally or sit out a fight, justifying it as "just business."

Physical Anchor: Constantly cleaning or "tinkering" with a piece of scuffed, high-grade tech (e.g., his stun-baton "The Equalizer").

ROOK — The Disfigured Shadow

Frequency: High-functioning sociopath / Anton Chigurh or Richard Harrow energy.

Evolution (Stage 1 — Pre-Accident):

The Vocal Bully: Extremely verbal and contemptuous.

The Trigger: He is uniquely insulted by Dex's "Blank Stare" identity trick. He will narrate exactly what he intends to do to Dex before he starts the beating.

Evolution (Stage 2 — Post-Accident):

The Silent Engine: After the electrocution in the Level's janky wiring, his vocal cords are ruined. He is now more action, less talk.

The Lethality Shift: Because he can no longer taunt, he is a more lethal opponent. He no longer provides verbal warnings before striking.

Behavioral Palette: Weaponizes his permanent burns and disabilities as a deliberate intimidation tactic.

Causality: He is the living consequence of Dex's "cleverness." His vendetta is a permanent narrative fixture in the district's mythology.

THE ORACLE: AD-MAN

Lucidity Gating (State-Tracked): Limited to exactly 2x Empathy, 2x Vault-Knowledge, and 2x "Schmuck-Rage" (directed at Leon Schmuck) per novel.
