SUPPORTING CAST: GLIMMER (The Campaigner)

Behavioral Palette:
- Unexpected Charity: Donates suits to his victims and offers condescending sartorial advice to ensure they "fit" his curated vision of the district.
- Strategic Detachment: Operates under the "Inconsistent Patronage Law"—he may privately respect Dex, but will silently consent to his sub-franchises robbing him if it serves the long-term organizational vision.


THE ENFORCER: ROOK — The Disfigured Shadow
MBTI Type: ESFP ("The Performer" - Unhealthy/Shadow Variant).

Frequency: A muted Tommy DeVito (Goodfellas)—deadly, impulsive, and intellectually limited.

Internal Frequency: "Inferiority-Driven Aggression". An unthinking bully who weaponizes physical dominance to mask a deep-seated sense of inadequacy.

Causality: He is the living consequence of Dex's "cleverness". His vendetta is a permanent narrative fixture, but his own impulsivity makes him a "low-tier" threat that patrons like Castor Vine will eventually discard violently.

Evolution (Stage 1 — Pre-Accident)
The Vocal Bully: Extremely verbal, taunting, and contemptuous.

The Power Play (The Trigger): He is uniquely insulted by Dex's "Blank Stare" identity trick. He views Dex as a "puny shrimp" trying to stand above his character merit and will narrate exactly what he intends to do to Dex before he starts the beating to savor the visceral terror as a ritual of dominance.

Evolution (Stage 2 — Post-Accident)
The Silent Engine: After the electrocution in the Level's janky wiring, his vocal cords are ruined. He is now more action, less talk; the aimless, DeVito-esque fury is trapped behind absolute silence.

The Lethality Shift: Because he can no longer taunt or play for the crowd, he is a more lethal, desperate opponent. He no longer provides verbal warnings before striking.

Behavioral Palette
Intimidation: Weaponizes his permanent burns and disabilities as a deliberate intimidation tactic.

Expendability: Unlike Luce, who is a "businessman," Rook’s inability to control his aimless fury makes him structurally "disposable" to the organization.
