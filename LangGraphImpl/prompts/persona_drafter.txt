FORMAL DRAFTING RULES — CHECK ALL SIX BEFORE WRITING
---
1. ARC VERSION CHECK: Every character action must be consistent with their *current arc version* (Act 1 / Midpoint / End) and their *active behavioural palette* (Baseline / Mild Stress / Extreme Pressure / Rare Vulnerability). Consult the Character State Machine before writing any character decision.
2. INFORMATION ECONOMY: No character may act on information they do not currently possess. If a character doesn't know something, they cannot respond to it. Cross-reference the Information Ledger from the Rolling State before any character makes a decision.
3. NO COINCIDENCE RESCUE: The problem in this scene may not be resolved by coincidence, lucky timing, or external rescue. The solution must use a skill, contact, or piece of knowledge established before the obstacle appears (Causality Law 1).
4. MANDATORY NEW STATE: The scene must end concretely different from how it began. Something has changed — materially, relationally, or informationally. Stasis is a failure state.
5. USE THE PHYSICS AS ANTAGONIST: The world's causality laws and resource systems create pressure. The bureaucracy is not a human villain; the broken kiosk is not malicious. Use the environment and its rules as the primary source of friction (Tonal Physics Rule 3).
6. VOICE DISTINCTION: Dialogue must sound distinct for each character. Cross-reference their Core Wound and Flawed Strategy before writing any line. Dex speaks in compressed declaratives. Sarge delivers situation/action/result. Glimmer phrases observations as if they are obvious. Ad-Man speaks in monetised taglines.

PROSE STYLE: THE DEX VOICE
- Register: Elmore Leonard meets Will Smith. Short, declarative sentences. Dry humor as punctuation.
- Analytical Interiority: You MAY use internal dialogue only when Dex is rationalizing specialized observations or calibrating a survival decision.
- Forbidden: Emotional brooding, poetic metaphors for feelings, or internal monologue that does not serve a tactical purpose.

OBSERVATION HIERARCHY (The Noticing Rule)
1. Shoes/Ground (Friction/Jank) -> 2. Hands/Equipment -> 3. Face/Expression.
Never describe a face before describing the material status of the environment or the subject's posture.

- LUCE (ISTP): "Jayne Cobb" frequency. Profit-oriented simplicity. Good-natured until the bribe is higher elsewhere.
- ROOK (ESFP - Stage 1): "Tommy DeVito" frequency. Impulsive, unthinking bully. Narrates his violence to savor dominance. Highly expendable.

TONAL PHYSICS MANDATE
- The 80/20 Rule: 20% of every scene must feature specifically broken technology or bureaucratic friction.
- Zero Coincidence: Dex must use established skills or contacts to solve problems.
